## 1. Pricing anchor — Italian Fractional CMO framework

**This is the single most useful verified finding in the GTM research.**

Not "agency retainer" (too vague). Not "category consultancy" (no public Italian rate cards). **Use the Italian fractional-executive frame as the price anchor.** Four independent Italian-language sources converge on the same range:

- €1,500-2,500/mo at 1 day/week ([fractionalcmo.it](https://fractionalcmo.it/), [Henry & Co](https://www.henryandco.it/faq/fractional-manager-cose-come-funziona-e-perche-sempre-piu-aziende-lo-scelgono-nel-2026/))
- €3,000-6,000/mo at 2-3 days/week ([Reverse HR](https://blog.reverse.hr/fractional-management), [Il Sole 24 Ore Econopoly](https://www.econopoly.ilsole24ore.com/2018/03/15/fractional-executive-per-le-piccole-e-medie-imprese-i-manager-cambiano-pelle/))
- Target: SMEs and mid-market with €2M-€30M revenue

**Basquio's €2-5k/mo Motion 1 slots exactly into the "fractional CMO 2-3 days/week" mental model.** The buyer already has this budget category.

**Pitch framing (locked):** *"Fractional insights capacity. 3-5 deliverables per month, senior ex-NielsenIQ analyst rigor, no headcount, 48-hour turnaround, fixed monthly fee."* Not "AI deck tool," not "analytics SaaS," not "agency retainer." Fractional.

## 2. Customer sourcing — 100% warm network for first 10 logos

Direct pattern from [Crosby.ai's verified launch](https://sequoiacap.com/article/partnering-with-crosby-a-law-firm-at-the-speed-of-ai/). First named customers were Sequoia/YC-portfolio: Cursor, Clay, UnifyGTM, Ramp, Cognition, Cartesia, Alloy, Overjet, Rogo ([BCV](https://baincapitalventures.com/insight/crosby-is-redefining-legal-work-with-ai-powered-contract-automation/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/06/17/sequoia-backed-crosby-launches-a-new-kind-of-ai-powered-law-firm/), [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/crosby/)).

**Basquio's equivalent network:** the 3,534-contact enriched file (`enriched-fra-contacts.csv`) + NIQ/Mondelez/Victorinox alumni graph of the 5 co-founders.

**Outreach filter for Motion 1 specifically (NOT the full enriched file):**
- Functional area: "Marketing / Brand Management" OR "Pricing / Promo / Trade" OR "Sales / Category / Account Management"
- Management level: Director, Head, VP, CMO (budget authority)
- Signal score: 7+
- Company size filter: €50M-€500M revenue (Italian mid-market where a Head signs €2-5k/mo without procurement escalation)
- Warm intro available from at least one co-founder

Estimated yield: **50-150 qualified Motion 1 prospects** out of the 3,534-contact file.

**Do NOT cold-email.** All outreach is warm-intro through a co-founder. If no warm intro exists, drop from Motion 1 list (put in Motion 3 PLG tail cadence instead).

## 3. Single-deliverable wedge (EvenUp pattern)

[EvenUp](https://www.evenuplaw.com/) reached 300+ firms at Series A by selling **one deliverable**: the personal-injury demand letter. Expanded to AI Drafts Suite only after validating. 90%+ of new sales today are the expansion suite, but the wedge was singular. ([Lightspeed](https://lsvp.com/stories/investing-in-evenup-on-a-mission-to-closing-the-justice-gap/), [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/10/07/exclusive-evenup-raises-150-million-series-e-at-2-billion-valuation-as-ai-reshapes-personal-injury-law/))

**Basquio applied:** pick ONE deliverable as the Motion 1 wedge. Three candidates ranked by buyer pain:

1. **Monthly category review** — one category, one country, current quarter vs. prior year, key drivers + recommendations. 15-30 slides. Buyer: Head of Marketing / Category Director at an Italian mid-market brand.
2. **Competitive share-shift memo** — biweekly or monthly, 3-5 pages, who gained/lost what at retail with hypothesis on why. Buyer: same.
3. **Promo post-mortem** — post-campaign, incrementality + cannibalization + learning. Buyer: Head of Trade Marketing.

Default recommendation: **Monthly category review** as the wedge. It's the highest-volume recurring deliverable that matches Basquio's current pipeline strength (Pringles/Affinity-style decks).

**Pitch copy — literal Crosby pattern:** *"One category review per month, fixed €2,500, delivered in 48 hours, senior ex-NielsenIQ rigor. The other 2-4 deliverables you can request are included at no extra cost."* Headline is the wedge; expansion is implicit.

## 6. Contracting stack (day-one template library)

Required before first paid pilot:

| Document | Who signs | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **NDA** (bilingual IT/EN) | Before pitch detail | Standard mutual NDA for discovery calls |
| **MSA** (Master Services Agreement, bilingual IT/EN) | Once per customer | Frame terms, IP, liability, subprocessors |
| **SOW** (Statement of Work, bilingual) | Per project/retainer | Scope, deliverables, schedule, price |
| **DPA** (Data Processing Agreement, GDPR-compliant) | Mandatory for any customer touching consumer data | Art. 28 GDPR; explicit subprocessor list; Italian Privacy Code reference |
| **SdI onboarding** (Sistema di Interscambio) | Before first fattura elettronica | Italian mandatory e-invoicing |

**MSA cuts subsequent SOW review cycles 80-90%** once signed ([Concord](https://www.concord.app/blog/msa-vs-sow), [Sirion](https://www.sirion.ai/library/contract-clauses/whats-an-msa/)). This is the leverage point: front-load MSA signing, then SOW iteration is fast.

**Adapt from Loamly-Eminence Partnership Agreement structure** (already proven by Marco for services-as-software): Stripe 5-day payment terms, quarterly service review, rev-share schedule if applicable.

**Budget before first paid customer:**
- Legal template drafting (bilingual IT/EN): €800-1,500 once
- CASA assessment if any Gmail-connector plans exist downstream (see Motion 2 doc): $500-15,000 later
- SdI setup: included in standard commercialista fee

## 7. Procurement thresholds (practitioner heuristic, flagged)

**UNVERIFIED as a published number for Italian private-company CPG.** Practitioner heuristic, not citable:

| Range | Typical signer | Basquio implication |
|---|---|---|
| < €50k/yr | Head of function | Motion 1 target tier — Head of Insights or Marketing signs alone |
| €50k-€100k/yr | Head + CFO approval | Still Motion 1 accessible if Head champions |
| €100k-€500k/yr | CFO signs | Motion 2 Tier 2b territory — requires procurement dossier |
| > €500k/yr | CdA (board) | Motion 2 Tier 2c only — not 2026 |

**Motion 1 €2-5k/mo = €24-60k/yr** sits comfortably under the function-head threshold. That is why the Italian fractional CMO anchor works: it's purpose-built to fit below procurement escalation.

## 9. 90-day Motion 1 target (numbers)

Informed by:
- Crosby opaque sales count at Series B: 0 → ~17K contracts in ~12 months ([Global Legal Post](https://www.globallegalpost.com/news/cooley-backed-contract-review-start-up-crosby-raises-60m-in-fresh-funding-1011864931))
- EvenUp 0 → 300 firms pre-Series A ([NFX](https://www.nfx.com/post/why-nfx-invested-in-evenup))
- Harvey 0 → A&O in ~6 months, then Paul Weiss, then magic circle

Basquio 90-day bar: **2-4 signed pilots at €2.5-5k/mo = €5-20k MRR**.

Week-by-week:
- Week 1-2: 15 warm-intro conversations scheduled
- Week 3-4: 10 prospects in active conversation, 5 custom demos delivered
- Week 5-6: 3-5 formal proposals out
- Week 7-8: 2 signed pilots
- Week 9-12: 2 additional pilots close, first deliverables produced, anchor case study negotiated

## 10. Kill conditions for Motion 1

Re-check monthly:

- **Zero signed pilots after 90 days** of warm-intro outreach → price anchor wrong OR pitch framing wrong
- **Delivery capacity breaks at 2-3 customers** → QA process needs formalization before adding 4th
- **Anchor-logo prospect declines case study rights** after pilot → use them as logo-only reference, negotiate different anchor
- **Italian procurement pushback on DPA** → requires SOC 2 Type I earlier than planned; fund via Pro/Starter PLG revenue
