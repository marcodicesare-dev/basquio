## 1. RGM Frameworks and Operating Models

### 1.1 Core RGM Levers (consensus across all firms)

The industry consensus identifies **five interlocking RGM levers**:

1. **Pricing** (base/list pricing, EDLP, dynamic pricing)
2. **Promotions** (trade promotions, TPR, multi-buy, display, feature)
3. **Assortment / Mix** (SKU rationalization, portfolio optimization)
4. **Price-Pack Architecture (PPA)** (pack size, format, price-point design)
5. **Trade Terms / Trade Investment** (gross-to-net management, retailer contracts)

Sources: McKinsey (4 levers: pricing, assortment, promotions, trade investment), Bain (pricing, assortment, PPA, promotions, trade terms), Deloitte (pricing, promotions, product assortment, brand-pack price architecture, gross-to-net), Kearney (pricing, pack-price architecture, promotion, mix, trade terms).

### 1.2 Named Frameworks

| Framework | Source | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Trifecta Approach** | Bain | Maximize value simultaneously for consumers, retailers, and manufacturers. Three must-have KPIs: trade profit, consumer value, 360-degree manufacturer ROI. |
| **Precision RGM** | McKinsey | Four maturity levels for promotion capabilities: (1) Basic ROI, (2) Standard optimization, (3) Promotion-impact simulation, (4) Household penetration + microtargeting |
| **Modern vs Traditional RGM** | Deloitte | Shift from rudimentary single-metric approaches to balanced scorecarding, portfolio mindset, sell-out insistence |
| **Three-Speed RGM** | PwC | Segment markets into Developed (AI-enabled insights), Semi-Developed (hybrid trade), Developing (visibility-first) |
| **RGM Maturity Index** | Bain | Proprietary index measuring RGM capabilities based on 200+ benchmarks |
| **Six Interlocking Elements** | Bain | (1) Transparency & alignment, (2) Operating model, (3) Path to excellence, (4) Tools & data power, (5) Convert insights into results, (6) Ensure repeatability & continuous improvement |
| **Balanced Scorecarding** | Deloitte | Multi-metric evaluation of promotions considering performance, strategy, and retailer outcome |
| **Market Archetype Segmentation** | PwC | Classify markets by 6 characteristics: trade classification, route-to-market complexity, data availability, financial controls, regulatory environment, local RGM talent |

### 1.3 RGM Maturity Stages (composite)

**Level 1 - Basic**: Spreadsheet-based, single-metric, sell-in data, gut-feel planning
**Level 2 - Standard**: Basic ROI tracking, internal data, limited promotion optimization
**Level 3 - Advanced**: Predictive simulators, multi-metric balanced scorecards, sell-out data, cross-portfolio analysis
**Level 4 - Precision**: Household-level penetration analysis, microtargeting, loyalty-card data integration, real-time AI-enabled optimization

Source: McKinsey promotion maturity model; nearly half of large European CPGs have no TPO tool; most companies stall at Level 2.

## 2. Gross-to-Net Waterfall Components

### 2.1 Full Waterfall Line Items (from TrewUp/McKinsey trade metrics)

**Gross Revenue** (total invoiced dollars before any deductions)

**Contra Revenue (Above the Line)**:
- Cash Term Discounts
- Slotting Fees
- Manufacturer Chargebacks (MCBs)
- Off-Invoice Discounts
- EDLPs (Everyday Low Prices)
- Fair Share
- Spoilage Allowances
- Short Shipments
- Returns

**Cost of Sales (Above the Line)**:
- Freight and Delivery Costs
- Damaged Goods
- Shipping Penalties
- Fulfillment Fees

= **Net Revenue** (Gross Revenue minus Contra Revenue)

= **Gross Profit** (Net Revenue minus Cost of Sales)

**Below the Line (SG&A)**:
- Retailer Ads
- Coupons
- Displays
- Admin Fees

### 2.2 Trade Spend Categories

**Working Trade Spend** (directly influences consumer purchase):
- Scan-based discounts (TPRs / Temporary Price Reductions)
- Retailer ads
- Coupons
- Display programs

**Non-Working Trade Spend** (supports shelf access, does not influence consumer):
- Spoilage
- Distributor programs (e.g., "Fair Share")
- Administrative fees

**Benchmark**: Non-working trade typically represents ~20% of total trade spend. For every $10M in trade, ~$2M may go to non-converting programs.

**Trade Deductions (Promotional)**:
- Promotions, EDLPs, Display Fees, Promotional Admin Fees, Advertising, Fair Share

**Non-Trade Deductions (Operational)**:
- Logistics Fees, Spoils, Shortages, Fines and Penalties

**Gray area**: Slotting Fees -- technically trade-related but often carved into a separate budget.

## 3. Trade Spend / Promotion Optimization KPIs

### 3.1 Core Trade KPIs (with definitions)

| KPI | Definition | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Trade Rate** | Total Trade Spend / Gross Revenue (expressed as %) | TrewUp |
| **Blended Trade Rate** | Total trade spend as % of revenue over a set period (quarter/year), smoothing across events | TrewUp |
| **Trade Promotion ROI** | (Incremental Gross Margin - Promotional Spend) / Promotional Spend | Visualfabriq |
| **True ROI** | Incremental net revenue / total event spend; breakeven threshold = 1.0 | TrewUp |
| **Incremental Sales** | Actual promoted volume minus modeled baseline volume | Multiple |
| **Incremental Revenue** | Incremental volume x net price realized | Multiple |
| **Contribution Margin ROI** | Incremental contribution margin after all discounts and deductions / total spend | TPO guide |
| **Account Profitability** | Net profit per account after deducting trade spend and related expenses | Visualfabriq |
| **Event Spend ROI** | Incremental revenue from non-promoted activities / total event spend | Visualfabriq |
| **Promotion Effectiveness** | Sales uplift during promotions, promotion ROI, customer acquisition rates | Visualfabriq |
| **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)** | Total expected revenue from a customer throughout the relationship | Visualfabriq |
| **Sales Uplift** | % increase in volume/revenue during promotional period vs baseline | Multiple |
| **New-to-Brand Rate** | % of promotional buyers who are new to the brand | TPO guide |
| **Repeat Rate** | % of promo buyers who repurchase within 8-12 weeks | TPO guide |
| **Display/Feature Compliance** | % of planned displays/features actually executed in-store | TPO guide |
| **OOS During Event** | % out-of-stock during promotional window | TPO guide |
| **Halo Effect** | Positive volume lift on adjacent SKUs/categories from a promotion | TPO guide |
| **Cannibalization Rate** | % of promotional lift that came at the expense of sibling SKUs | TPO guide |
| **Deduction Recovery Cycle Time** | Days to resolve/recover trade deductions | TPO guide |
| **Retailer Margin** | Margin earned by retailer on promoted events | Bain |
| **Retailer Passthrough** | Amount of price support actually passed through to the consumer | Deloitte |
| **Promo/Event ROI** | Revenue impact evaluation ensuring event spending doesn't drive negative portfolio impact | Deloitte |

### 3.2 TPM vs TPO Distinction

- **TPM (Trade Promotion Management)**: Plans, budgets, contracts, claims tracking
- **TPO (Trade Promotion Optimization)**: Predicts, simulates, improves incremental impact; reallocates spend from weak to strong events

## 4. Joint Business Planning (JBP) Frameworks

### 4.1 Deloitte JBP Framework ("Winning with Retail")

Based on Deloitte's 2026 Retail-CPG Commercial Collaboration Benchmark Study:

**Key findings**:
- JBP rated as the most impactful area of collaboration by both retail and CPG respondents
- Yet remains one of the least transformational in practice
- 73% of companies increased collaboration over the past 5 years
- 86% saw higher sales from collaboration
- Companies want to collaborate 1.2x-2x more than they do today
- Advanced collaboration can deliver **mid-single-digit top-line growth**

**Five Collaboration Opportunity Areas**:
1. **Joint Business Planning (JBP)**: Redefine as real-time alignment system with shared data, transparent targets, AI-enabled decision-making
2. **Revenue Growth Management (RGM)**: Align on common RGM vision for price perception, promo efficiency, category growth
3. **Innovation**: Joint ambition-setting, first-party data sharing, AI-enabled concepting
4. **Route to Market (RTM)**: Shared view of priorities, lower-cost/higher-service value chain
5. **Talent**: Free teams from capacity bottlenecks to focus on value creation

**JBP Perception Gap**: ~90% of retailers want stronger collaboration with CPG partners, but CPGs perceive "lack of desire" from retailers as the top barrier. Capacity constraints, not willingness, are the true culprit.

### 4.2 Operationalizing TPO in JBP (TPO guide)

- Arrive at JBP with **3 pre-simulated promotion options per window**, each with modeled incremental units, retailer margin, and category impact
- Set joint targets for on-time execution and on-shelf availability
- Publish one-page recap after each event with net incrementality and agreed next steps
- Key JBP KPIs: incremental units, new-to-brand rate, contribution margin, display compliance

### 4.3 Bain "Customer GM Mindset"

Multifunctional teams sharing the ultimate goal of growing the **joint profit pool** of both retailer and manufacturer. RGM insights must be realistic and flexible, not just analytically elegant.

## 6. Operating Model Design Principles

### 6.1 Kearney RGM Operating Model (2025 Brief, n=51 CPG practitioners)

**Three design moves for fully enabled organizations**:

**Move 1: Give RGM a seat at the table**
- 71% of global RGM leads sit at same level as P&L owner or one step below
- 84% at regional levels, 88% at market levels
- 56% report to business profit center (not functional cost center)
- Leading orgs: 85%+ report directly to or one level below P&L owners
- Two-thirds of regional organizations combine RGM and commercial development responsibilities

**Move 2: Build capacity where it can scale**
- Leading organizations: **5.9 RGM FTEs per 1,000 employees** (2.3x that of progressing companies at 2.6)
- Tiered operating model:
  - **Global centers**: Define frameworks, guardrails, analytical toolkits
  - **Regional hubs**: Apply frameworks, define value creation opportunities, drive adoption (~21% of RGM staff)
  - **Shared services centers**: Data processing, automation, analysis, reporting at scale (~10% of RGM staff)
  - **Local teams**: Apply plays to local consumers and customers (<50% of RGM staff in leading orgs)

**Move 3: Embed RGM early in value definition**
- Leading companies involve RGM in core innovation pricing and commercial innovation
- Greater integration in financial planning and portfolio management
- Opportunity: tactical S&OP, where RGM often plays limited role despite relevance

### 6.2 EY RGM Operating Model

- Cross-functional approach: CCO, CIO, CFO as key stakeholders
- **Center of Excellence (CoE)** model for multi-market execution, sharing technology + skills + learnings
- Technology that decouples data from analytics, supporting entire Integrated Business Planning (IBP) cycle
- Change management with communications, stakeholder engagement, business readiness, training

### 6.3 PwC Operating Model Principles

- **RGM-as-a-Service**: Shared global expertise, centralized analytics, locally deployed insights
- Regional Centers of Excellence to lift capability across adjacent markets
- RGM embedded as cross-functional capability within finance, sales, and category management
- Segment markets by archetype, not just geography

### 6.4 Bain Operating Model Principles

- **Customer GM mindset**: Multifunctional teams sharing goal of growing joint profit pool
- Build on existing commercial routines (don't redesign everything)
- Upgrade performance management KPIs to ensure RGM woven into customer planning
- End-user-back technology design: save time for commercial front line, connect to existing processes
- MVS (Minimum Viable Solution) approach: launch quickly in test markets, then scale

### 6.5 Key Governance Metrics (Kearney survey)

- 67% of CEOs say RGM is a key enabler of financial performance
- Only 33% of RGM leads feel equipped to perform at full potential
- 50%+ of world's top 20 CPGs describe RGM as central to earnings delivery
- 80%+ CPGs highlight RGM as important (Deloitte analysis of public comments)
- 80%+ CP chief executives dissatisfied with their RGM results (Bain)
- 51% of CPGs say increased marketing/advertising/promotional spending is top investment priority (KPMG 2024)

### 6.6 Five Barriers to Scaling (Kearney)

1. **Technology & data foundations** (50% cite as major challenge)
2. **Capability gaps** (only 14% have scaled RGM upskilling; of those, just 14% rate content as high quality)
3. **Capacity constraints** (83% of leading firms cite as top-2 barrier)
4. **Process integration** (9 in 10 early-stage companies cite as key barrier)
5. **People/structural issues** (~33% cite, but often symptom of integration/ownership/automation gaps)

## 7. Price-Pack Architecture (PPA) Frameworks

### 7.1 PPA Four Pillars

1. **Price Elasticity Modeling**: Forecasts, optimized pricing, competitor awareness
2. **Value Perception Scoring**: Aligned features, effective communication, customer focus
3. **Competitive Positioning**: Competitive advantages, effective countermoves, market awareness
4. **Consumer Willingness to Pay (WTP)**: Accurate estimation, revenue maximization, informed product development

### 7.2 PPA Implementation Steps (7-step process)

1. **Category Deep Dive**: Map competitive landscape, track price-pack trends, identify white spaces, analyze consumer behavior
2. **Value Proposition Development**: Map consumer decision hierarchy, identify key value drivers, track emerging need states
3. **Price Pack Design**: Create distinct value propositions, cover all key price points, build flexibility for future innovation
4. **Financial Modeling**: Model complete P&L impact, account for cross-SKU cannibalization, factor competitor responses, build sensitivity analysis
5. **Go-to-Market**: Clear value communication, phased implementation, robust tracking, internal alignment
6. **Continuous Optimization**: Monitor performance, track consumer response, watch competitor moves, adjust quickly

### 7.3 PPA Strategy by Market Type (PwC/Kearney)

- **Developed markets**: AI-enabled insights, mid-tier customer modeling, nuanced pricing; discount stacking rules vary by country
- **Semi-developed markets**: Mix of modern and traditional trade; segment shoppers and channels precisely; sachet economy coexists with premiumization
- **Developing markets**: Visibility is victory; multilayered distribution; balance top-down standards with on-the-ground realities

### 7.4 Channel-Specific PPA

- **E-commerce**: Convenience packs, club packs for pantry-loaders; price transparency creates channel conflict challenges
- **Mass retail**: Value packs, multi-buy formats
- **Premium channels**: Experience-led packs
- **Quick commerce**: Trial/impulse packs (e.g., INR 20 trial packs driving 30% repeat purchase)

### 7.5 PPA Benchmarks

- Enhanced PPA can increase EBIT margins by up to **4 percentage points** (Roland Berger)
- 70% of consumers are more price-conscious, yet 67% still willing to pay more for products delivering genuine value
- 90% of product launches fail to meet financial targets (often linked to ineffective pricing)
- Unilever: weather-based demand forecasting for ice cream yielded **~30% sales increase** in certain markets

## 8. AI and Technology in RGM

### 8.1 AI Adoption (Kearney 2025)

- **53%** of organizations have AI pilots in RGM (mostly small-scale)
- Only **~10%** report systematic, scaled AI rollouts across markets
- 40%+ operate a centralized data lake; ~60% among advanced players
- 49% have global product owners for RGM technology

**AI pilot focus areas** (ranked):
1. Data analysis (low-code/no-code tools for visualization, interpretation, automation)
2. Data integration (visual recognition of retailer contracts, syndicated data lake)
3. Recommendation engines
4. Chatbots/copilots for natural language recognition

### 8.2 Three Moves to Scale AI in RGM (Kearney)

1. **Codified RGM process blueprint**: Common strategy, KPIs, planning rhythms
2. **Governed single source of truth**: Clean, connected data layer with shared semantics, KPI definitions, automated quality checks
3. **Embed product ownership into delivery**: Accountability, investment, and place in operating model

### 8.3 AI Applications in PPA and Trade

- **Demand Forecasting & Elasticity Modeling**: Forecast demand for different pack sizes/price tiers
- **Trade Investment Optimization**: Model which promotions work best for which pack/price combinations in which channels
- **Hyper-Personalized Pack & Price Recommendations**: GenAI integrating purchase history, basket size, income tiers, channel preferences
- **Predictive Promotion Simulators**: Starting from past observations, automatically build patterns of future promo efficiency

### 8.4 EY Technology Approach

- **Consumer Commercial Analytics Platform**: AI-driven, learns from POS, weather, events data
- Decouples data from analytics (both evolve independently)
- Supports demand planning, financial forecasting, and entire IBP cycle

## 9. Key Formulas

**Trade Rate** = Trade Spend / Gross Revenue

**Trade Budget** = Target Trade Rate x Projected Revenue

**Trade Promotion ROI** = (Incremental Gross Margin - Promotional Spend) / Promotional Spend

**True ROI** = Incremental Net Revenue / Total Event Spend (breakeven = 1.0)

**Incremental Lift** = Actual Promoted Volume - Modeled Baseline Volume

**Blended Trade Rate** = Total Trade Spend / Total Revenue over a period

**Net Revenue** = Gross Revenue - Contra Revenue (discounts, allowances, returns)

**Gross Profit** = Net Revenue - Cost of Sales
