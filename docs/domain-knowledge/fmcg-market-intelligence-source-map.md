# FMCG Market Intelligence Source Map

Date researched: April 1, 2026

## Purpose

This file is the source-backed map for the broader FMCG / CPG / retail / consumer / consultant / finance knowledge upgrade.

It is not a runtime artifact.

It is the repo’s traceable index of public materials worth turning into future domain packs.

## Coverage logic

The goal was not “collect random articles.”

The goal was to map the strongest public signals for:

- retail measurement
- consumer panels
- shopper and occasion behavior
- category management
- pricing, promotion, assortment, and pack architecture
- revenue growth management
- joint business planning
- macro and consumer foresight
- consultant and commercial-finance operating logic

## Cross-firm takeaways

### Data foundations

- NIQ and Circana are the strongest public sources for “what happened” plus validated purchase behavior.
- Kantar adds stronger “how people shop / consume / choose” logic across purchase, usage, and occasions.
- GfK Consumer Life and Euromonitor are stronger for “what changes next” than for near-term retail tracking.

### Category and commercial execution

- Kantar, dunnhumby, NIQ, and Circana together cover category, assortment, shopper behavior, and retail execution better than any one firm alone.
- NIQ and Circana are measurement-heavy.
- dunnhumby is retailer / loyalty / personalization-heavy.
- Kantar is shopper / usage / panel / category-leadership-heavy.

### Finance and board logic

- McKinsey, Deloitte, Bain, PwC, EY, and Kearney are the highest-signal public sources for:
  - gross-to-net logic
  - trade investment
  - RGM operating models
  - P&L adjacency
  - AI-enabled planning and governance
  - CFO / CCO / CEO framing

### What this means for Basquio

The repo should not treat “FMCG domain knowledge” as one document family.

It should treat it as six connected source classes:

1. measurement and market truth
2. shopper and consumer behavior
3. category and retail execution
4. macro and future trends
5. consultant issue-framing
6. commercial finance and RGM operating logic
