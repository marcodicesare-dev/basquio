## Executive distillation

The deck teaches a structured storytelling system for insights work:

- Storytelling matters because it improves partnership quality, strategic influence, and budget support.
- The primary structure is `SCQA`:
  - `Situation`
  - `Complication`
  - `Question`
  - `Answer`
- The `Answer` should be quantified and action-oriented, not generic.
- The deck pairs `SCQA` with the `Pyramid Principle`, `POVs / Supporting Implications`, and a `What / So What / Now What` flow to turn raw findings into executive narratives.
- Data interpretation should explicitly look for:
  - `Connection`
  - `Contradiction`
  - `Curiosity`
- The presentation logic should choose between `deductive` and `inductive` storytelling depending on audience and meeting context.
- Recommendation quality depends on prioritization:
  - size of prize
  - realism
  - ease of execution
  - timeframe
  - fit with current plans
- AI is positioned as an assistive partner for testing and tightening the SCQA, not as a substitute for human judgment.

## Core concepts extracted from the deck

### 1. Storytelling is a commercial capability

The deck directly frames storytelling as a commercial tool:

- teams known for storytelling win more strategic projects
- teams that turn insights into actions are more likely to be endorsed during budget conversations
- strong storytelling and facilitation strengthen client partnerships

This matters for Basquio because it aligns with report-generation as decision support, not decorative slide production.

### 2. Great storytelling formula

The deck states:

- `SCQA + The Pyramid Principle (POV) = Storyboard`

Operationally that means:

- start from a business situation
- surface the real complication
- turn that into the true question
- answer it with quantified, implementable action
- support the answer with points of view and evidence

### 3. A strong SCQA is specific, commercial, and quantified

The Alexia example is one of the clearest slides in the PDF.

Weak/original form:

- volume is declining
- we do not know why
- what is causing the decline
- decline is due to distribution and loyalty loss

Improved form:

- decline is tied to spend shifting to value brands
- the brand wants to reverse the decline and boost sales by a target by year-end
- buyers may be leaving because availability is weak
- the question becomes where the biggest opportunities are to reverse decline and grow sales
- the answer becomes a quantified plan involving buyer recovery, purchase frequency, retailer listings, and bigger pack considerations

This is especially relevant to FMCG / CPG work because it grounds the answer in:

- buyers
- distribution
- retailer listings
- pack architecture
- growth target

### 4. Questions to ask when building the SCQA

The deck provides explicit prompting questions.

For `Situation`:

- What is the ambition for the brand?
- Why is it important?
- What does success look like?
- How does it link to strategic priorities?
- What actions have been taken already?

For `Complication`:

- What is the issue?
- How did it come up?
- What impact is it having?

For `Question`:

- What is critical versus nice to know?
- What is feasible to answer from the data?

For `Answer`:

- What hypotheses do you have?
- Why do you think that is the reason?
- Are there limits to the potential recommendations?
- What is viable to action?

### 5. SCQA is not just for decks

The deck shows SCQA can be used:

- in conversation
- in email proposals
- in thought leadership
- with clients who do not know the framework

This matters because later Basquio usage could apply the same logic to:

- deck planning
- brief clarification
- report summary pages
- client-facing proposal surfaces

### 6. Data interpretation should search for three patterns

The deck says strong analysis should look for:

- `Connection`: findings that support what is already known or hypothesized
- `Contradiction`: findings that challenge what is already known or hypothesized
- `Curiosity`: unexpected findings that open a new angle

This is a strong candidate for future deterministic or semi-deterministic analysis heuristics.

### 7. Top-down and bottom-up must meet

The deck describes a three-part data sensemaking loop:

1. briefing-stage SCQA creates a top-down plan
2. bottom-up analysis generates findings
3. interpretation compares both and turns the result into story

The stated goal is to avoid getting lost in the data while still letting real findings shape the narrative.

### 8. Deductive vs inductive storytelling

The deck distinguishes:

- `Inductive`: context first, answer last
- `Deductive`: answer first, then supporting evidence

It recommends:

- deductive when the audience is short on time, needs prioritization, or is senior
- inductive when the audience needs more context, wants to see the data build, or recommendations depend on background setup

### 9. Pyramid Principle and POVs

The deck frames the pyramid as:

- `Level 1`: answer
- `Level 2`: points of view / supporting implications
- `Level 3`: key evidence

POVs are described as:

- synthesized interpretation
- the bridge from raw findings to recommendation
- a way to reduce cognitive overload by doing the recoding for the audience

The deck repeatedly stresses that a POV is not just one fact. It is a higher-level interpretive statement.

### 10. What / So What / Now What

The deck translates narrative into three functions:

- `What`: descriptive evidence and context
- `So What`: analysis, implication, risk, or opportunity
- `Now What`: action and forward-looking recommendation

This is one of the cleanest Basquio-ready abstractions in the file.

### 11. Data overload management

The deck explicitly says:

- `Golden Rule: Less is better`
- differentiate `nice to know` from `need to know`
- invest time upfront to refine the `SCQ` and hypothetical answer
- analyze in cycles
- deep-dive enough to avoid missing something vital

### 12. Prioritize recommendations

The deck offers a prioritization lens:

- size of prize
- realism
- ease of execution
- timeframe
- fit with current direction

That is useful as a future recommendation-ranking heuristic.

### 13. AI usage guidance

The AI section is modest and pragmatic:

- human-in-the-loop creates the magic
- distinguish human-led / AI-assisted versus AI-led / human-taught activities
- use Copilot to test and refine an SCQA
- iterate prompts until the question and answer are commercially correct

The Pampers Splashers example shows an important pattern:

- first AI answer improved wording but not the commercial framing
- user then corrected the real question
- second answer better reflected the true sell-in objective

This is a strong reminder that the human must define the actual commercial ask.

## Basquio-relevant extraction: what to reuse later

If this content is later turned into a Basquio domain pack, the highest-value reusable rules are:

- Start from the business issue, not from the dataset.
- Turn descriptive findings into an `answerable executive question`.
- Use findings to build `POVs`, not just fact dumps.
- Force a `What / So What / Now What` ladder.
- Prefer `deductive` storytelling for senior, time-poor audiences.
- Treat `connection / contradiction / curiosity` as a structured interpretation pass.
- Keep recommendations prioritized by value, feasibility, speed, and strategic fit.
- Use AI to refine framing, not to outsource the commercial logic.
