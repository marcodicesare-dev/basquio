## What This Deck Is

This is a Nielsen BASES training deck on innovation fundamentals and launch evaluation. It is not a generic creativity deck. Its operating model is:

1. decide where growth should come from
2. discover the strongest propositions
3. develop product, pack, and communication
4. deploy in market and track launch performance

For Basquio, this deck is most useful as a reusable knowledge pack for:

- innovation strategy narratives
- pre-launch test design
- launch KPI framing
- post-launch performance tracking
- cannibalization and incrementality analysis
- recommendation logic for innovation bets

## High-Signal Themes

### 1. Innovation exists to grow the brand, not to create noise

Early slides frame innovation as a growth engine rather than a retailer-compliance exercise. The deck shows that most executives launch new products to grow the brand, while very few do it to satisfy retailer expectations or simply feed sales teams.

Basquio implication:

- recommendation logic should treat innovation as a business growth lever
- weak launches should be framed as poor growth allocation, not just execution misses

### 2. Success criteria are inconsistent in the market

The deck explicitly says launch success is defined in multiple ways and highlights the need for a consistent scorecard.

Basquio implication:

- when building innovation decks, force a clear definition of success:
  - sales level
  - distribution build
  - trial
  - repeat
  - awareness / brand effect
  - incrementality
  - forecast vs actual

### 3. BASES innovation process = Decide / Discover / Develop / Deploy

This is the clearest reusable structure in the document.

- `Decide`: identify growth spaces and strategic priorities
- `Discover`: shape the proposition and screen opportunities
- `Develop`: refine product, pack, and communication
- `Deploy`: activate in market and evaluate launch performance

Basquio implication:

- innovation-related decks should often map naturally into these four stages
- slide planning can use this as a section spine when the brief is new-product or launch oriented

## Section Map

### Pages 1-13: Innovation purpose, success, and BASES overview

Core ideas:

- why brands launch products
- how often launches meet expectations
- why success needs a clear definition
- overview of the BASES process and services

Reusable knowledge:

- launch programs need explicit outcome definitions
- executive stories should move from business objective to innovation system

### Pages 14-25: Pre-launch logic and predictive analytics

Core ideas:

- pre-launch work should maximize return on innovation
- good innovation is grounded in predictive analytics
- Nielsen BASES uses proprietary models and validated frameworks
- forecasting is built like a simulated test market
- target-group analysis and foundational models are validated

Reusable knowledge:

- pre-launch decks should separate concept strength from execution readiness
- predictive analytics can be framed as risk reduction, not only forecast generation
- target group fit is a core gate before launch

### Pages 26-44: Activation profiles and strategic archetypes

This is one of the richest sections.

The deck describes different activation profiles tied to strategic intent:

- `Growers`: grow the brand by stealing share or changing the category
- `Defenders`: reinforce the core and defend the brand
- `Explorers`: create or explore new territory
- profile examples include:
  - Superstar
  - Brand Grower
  - Crowd Pleaser
  - Short-Term Play
  - Emerging Play
  - Targeted Play
  - Reconsider

Reusable knowledge:

- innovation should be classified by strategic role, not treated as one generic launch type
- different profiles imply different support needs, distribution expectations, and post-launch benchmarks

Basquio implication:

- innovation recommendation slides should explicitly classify bets into strategic roles
- portfolio decks can use these archetypes as a decision layer above raw sales

### Pages 45-60: Post-launch tracking and benchmarking

Core ideas:

- weekly tracking of sales via retail data
- monthly trial and repeat views via home scan / consumer panel
- launches can be evaluated:
  - against standard indicators
  - against external benchmarks
  - against BASES forecast when available
- example KPIs include:
  - speed to shelf / availability
  - weighted distribution
  - volume share
  - buying households
  - repeaters

Reusable knowledge:

- post-launch reporting should compare actual performance both to category norms and to forecast
- launch underperformance must be decomposed into:
  - distribution / speed-to-shelf
  - trial
  - repeat
  - awareness / support

### Pages 61-80: Brand effect, new product alert, smart shopper, and sourcerer

This section links media, shopper behavior, and incrementality.

Core modules:

- `Brand Effect Analysis`: measures campaign impact on brand KPIs
- `New Product Alert`: captures early trier experience and launch diagnostics
- `Smart Shopper`: in-the-moment shopper insight
- `Sourcerer`: estimates source of volume after launch
  - incrementality
  - cannibalization
  - shifting from other brands / segments

Reusable knowledge:

- innovation tracking is not just sales reporting
- awareness drivers, shelf positioning, promo, trial, satisfaction, and source-of-volume all matter
- cannibalization and incrementality should be first-class outputs in launch decks

## Basquio-Useful Objects

### Strategic Questions

- Is the launch meant to grow the core, defend the base, or create a new space?
- Is weak performance caused by concept weakness, execution weakness, or support weakness?
- Is the launch growing the franchise or just shifting volume internally?
- Is actual performance above or below forecast? Why?

### Diagnostic Modules

- launch objective alignment
- trial / repeat decomposition
- speed-to-shelf and weighted distribution
- benchmark vs forecast comparison
- awareness driver diagnostics
- source-of-volume and cannibalization

### Common Output Shapes

- pre-launch scorecard
- activation profile classification
- forecast vs actual bridge
- launch KPI tracker
- source-of-volume waterfall
- driver table for awareness / trial / repeat
