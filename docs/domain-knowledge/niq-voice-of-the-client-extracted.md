## What This Deck Is

This is a Nielsen training deck about client needs, solution mapping, and how to translate business questions into the right analytical package.

It is not primarily a metric-methodology deck. It is a commercial and consulting logic deck that helps answer:

- what clients are really asking
- which NIQ solution family should answer each need
- how different datasets and methods combine across a project
- how to turn raw data capability into client-ready delivery

For Basquio, this is a high-value routing and packaging pack.

## High-Signal Themes

### 1. Start from the client question, not from the dataset

The deck is built around a basic consulting truth:

- clients ask business questions
- NIQ must map those questions to the right solutions

Examples of core client questions shown in the deck:

- how are my markets performing?
- what is my share versus competitors?
- where do I have distribution gaps?
- what are the main drivers of the trend?
- how should I think about pricing, promotion, assortment, innovation, and strategic scenarios?

Basquio implication:

- runs should begin from a business-question classification step, not immediately from metric production
- the chosen knowledge pack should follow the question, not just the uploaded file format

### 2. The NIQ offer is a portfolio organized around needs

The deck frames the NIQ offer as a portfolio spanning:

- economic environment
- scenario and strategic assessments
- retail tracking
- store audit
- consumer panel
- detailed ad hoc analysis
- automation and portability tools

Basquio implication:

- Basquio should behave like a solution router across NIQ-like question types
- the master knowledge graph should explicitly connect problem types to evidence packages and output shapes

### 3. Strategic work combines multiple lenses

The deck shows that strategic questions are not solved by one table or one standard view. For example:

- strategic assessment example:
  - is it worth entering Fabric Enhancers?
  - if yes, through which route?
  - revitalize an existing brand?
  - stretch a laundry brand?
  - launch a new brand?
  - acquire an existing brand?

This implies a multi-lens answer:

- market sizing
- competition
- barriers and opportunities
- trends
- innovation routes

Basquio implication:

- strategic decks should synthesize RMS, CPS, innovation, assortment, distribution, pricing, and competition rather than treating them as isolated chapters

### 4. Performance analysis is broader than trend charts

The performance section covers:

- RMS tracking
- market segmentation
- emerging trends
- consumer tracking
- demographics
- buyer-base evolution
- retail market breakdowns

Basquio implication:

- performance should be decomposed into:
  - market size and trend
  - segment trend
  - consumer mix
  - buyer-base evolution
  - geography / channel structure

### 5. New ways of working matter

The deck explicitly highlights:

- automation
- portability
- mobile access
- local apps
- RPA and AI

Basquio implication:

- the product should not just produce analysis; it should fit client operating constraints like speed, repeatability, and portability

### 6. Client-ready delivery is part of the value

The Montenegro case history is about report and presentation automation, customization, and time saving.

Basquio implication:

- artifact quality and repeatable delivery are not secondary polish; they are part of the solution promise

### 7. Marketing lever analysis is an integrated framework

The second half of the deck organizes work around:

- innovation
- sales decomposition
- distribution
- assortment
- pricing
- promotions

This is highly relevant for Basquio because it maps the main commercial levers into one integrated problem tree.
