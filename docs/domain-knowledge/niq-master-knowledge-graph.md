This graph is canonical in English. Italian terms are kept as aliases for parser/traceability only.

## NIQ Product Portfolio (Source: La Voce del Cliente)

### Tier 1: Tracking (continuous panel data)
- **RMS (Retail Measurement Services)**: store-level sales tracking across channels
  - Key outputs: Sales Value, Volume, Units, Share, Distribution, Price Index
  - Italian alias: "Tracking dei Panel" / "Retail tracking"
  - Channels: Iper, Super, Libero Servizio, Discount, Specialisti, Online
- **Consumer Panel (CPS)**: household-level purchase tracking via optical scanner
  - Key outputs: Penetration, Frequency, Basket Size, Loyalty, Trial/Repeat
  - 9,000 households, 25,000+ individuals aged 14+
  - Tracks both barcoded products AND fresh products
  - Italian alias: "Consumer Panel" / "Tracking dei consumi"

- **Category Management**: assortment, planogram, space optimization
- **Price & Promo Modeling**: baseline/incremental decomposition, price elasticity, TPR simulation
- **Micromarketing**: store-level targeting, geo-clustering

### Tier 3: Insights
- **CPS Surveys & Special Analyses**: shopper motivation, brand perception
- **CI & Shopper Studies**: custom research, path-to-purchase

### Tier 4: Strategic Context
- **Economic Environment / Scenarios**: macro indicators, GDP, inflation, consumer confidence
- **Global Reports**: cross-country benchmarks
- **Thought Leadership**: industry publications, conferences

## RMS Core Concepts

### Sales Decomposition
- **Sales Value (CY)**: Revenue in current year period. Italian: "V. Valore"
- **Sales Value (PY)**: Revenue in prior year same period. Italian: "V. Valore AA"
- **Sales Volume**: Physical volume (liters, kg). Italian: "V. Volume"
- **Sales Units**: Package count. Italian: "V. Confezioni"
- **Value Growth %**: (CY - PY) / PY * 100

### Share
- **Value Share**: Brand value / Total market value * 100
- **Share Change (pp)**: CY share - PY share
- Share MUST always specify denominator: "18.3% of Total Tracked Market"

### Price
- **Average Price per Unit**: Value / Units
- **Average Price per Volume**: Value / Volume
- **Price Index**: Brand avg price / Category avg price * 100
  - >100 = premium positioning
  - <100 = value positioning
- **Relative Price Position**: Brand price / Competitor price

### Distribution
- **Numeric Distribution**: % of stores carrying the product
- **Weighted Distribution (%ACV)**: % of category value in stores carrying the product
  - More meaningful than numeric: a product in 50% of stores by count might have 80% ACV
- **Distribution Gap**: Weighted dist. of product vs. benchmark (category, competitor, portfolio avg)
- **Void Analysis**: stores that carry the category but NOT the product

### Velocity
- **ROS (Rate of Sale)**: Sales per store per week (units or value)
  - Italian: "Vendita media per punto vendita"
- **Velocity = Distribution quality**: high dist + low ROS = over-distribution. Low dist + high ROS = under-distribution (opportunity).

### Promotion
- **Promo Intensity**: % of value sold on promotion
- **Baseline Sales**: Sales at regular price (modeled)
- **Incremental Sales**: Additional sales generated by promotion
- **Promo Lift**: Incremental / Baseline
- **TPR (Temporary Price Reduction)**: depth bands 10-20%, 20-30%, 30-40%, >40%
  - >40% = "desperate pricing", margin destruction
- **EDLP (Every Day Low Price)**: strategy where regular price IS the competitive price (Discount channel)

## Price & Promo Modeling (Source: Eduard deck)

### Sales Decomposition Model
Total Sales = Baseline Sales + Incremental Sales
- **Baseline**: what would sell without any promo (modeled via regression)
- **Incremental**: delta caused by promotional activity
- **Vendite Regolari**: sales at full shelf price (non-promotional)
- **Vendite Promozionate**: sales during promo periods

### Price Sensitivity
- **Price Elasticity**: % volume change / % price change
  - Elastic (>|1|): promo-responsive categories
  - Inelastic (<|1|): must-have categories, brand loyalty
- **Cross-Elasticity**: volume impact on competitors from own price change

### Scenario Simulation
- Model: "What if we reduce price by 5%?"
- Output: predicted volume uplift, margin impact, cannibalization estimate
- Used by trade marketing teams for promo calendar planning

## CPS Consumer Panel (Source: CPS 2023 Template)

### Purchase Metrics
- **Penetration**: % of households that bought at least once in period
- **Frequency**: average number of purchase occasions per buyer
- **Basket Size**: average spend or units per occasion
- **Loyalty (Share of Requirements)**: % of category spend going to the brand
- **Trial**: first-time buyers in period
- **Repeat**: buyers who purchase again within period
- **Retention Rate**: repeat buyers / trial buyers

### Dupont Decomposition (CPS)
Value = Penetration * Frequency * Basket Size * Price
- This decomposition explains WHY value changed
- "Penetration grew but frequency fell" = different strategy than "fewer buyers spending more"

### Shopper Segmentation
- By channel: Where do they buy?
- By frequency: Heavy/Medium/Light buyers
- By loyalty: Exclusive / Repertoire / Switchers
- By demographics: Age, income, household size, region

## Innovation / BASES (Source: Innovation Basic deck)

### Launch Metrics
- **Trial Volume**: projected first-year buyers
- **Repeat Rate**: % of triers who repurchase
- **Year 1 Revenue**: trial * repeat * basket * price
- **Incrementality**: % of launch volume that is NEW to the category (vs. cannibalization)
- **Source of Volume**: from competitor, from adjacent category, genuinely new occasions

### Innovation Assessment
- Concept testing (pre-launch): purchase intent, uniqueness, relevance
- In-market tracking (post-launch): actual trial, repeat, source of volume
- Benchmark: compare to category norms for trial/repeat

## Diagnostic Motif Library

### 1. availability_problem
- Signal: weighted distribution 15+ pts below benchmark, ROS at or above average
- Story: "Product sells where it's available, but isn't available where it should be"
- Recommendation: distribution expansion at specific retailers
- Exhibit: dist_vs_velocity_scatter or ranked_share_bar by retailer

### 2. velocity_problem
- Signal: weighted distribution comparable to benchmark, ROS 20%+ below average
- Story: "Product is present but not moving"
- Recommendation: pricing review, pack architecture, shelf positioning
- Exhibit: cy_vs_py_grouped by brand/SKU

### 3. price_mix_tension
- Signal: value growth > volume growth (price-driven), OR value decline > volume decline
- Story: "Growing value but losing volume signals unsustainable pricing"
- Recommendation: pack architecture changes, entry-point SKU launch
- Exhibit: pvm_waterfall or trend_line (value vs volume indexed)

### 4. promo_dependence
- Signal: promo intensity >50%, incremental/baseline ratio declining
- Story: "Dependent on promotions with diminishing returns"
- Recommendation: rebuild baseline through innovation, distribution, or brand investment
- Exhibit: promo_baseline_stack over time

### 5. portfolio_mismatch
- Signal: brand segment mix differs from category by >5pp
- Story: "Brand portfolio doesn't match where the category is growing"
- Recommendation: portfolio rebalancing toward growth segments
- Exhibit: mix_comparison_stack (brand vs category)

### 6. hero_concentration
- Signal: top 3 SKUs >50% of brand value
- Story: "Over-reliance on hero SKUs creates vulnerability"
- Recommendation: hero renovation + tail pruning + selective launches
- Exhibit: hero_concentration_pareto

### 7. share_erosion
- Signal: declining share in flat or growing category
- Story: "Losing ground while the market holds steady"
- Recommendation: competitive response (distribution, pricing, innovation)
- Exhibit: trend_line (share over time) or ranked_share_bar (vs competitors)

## FMCG Action Levers (for recommendations)

Every recommendation must use a specific FMCG lever:

1. **Distribution**: "List [SKU] in [retailer] to gain [X] pts ACV and capture €[Y]M"
2. **Pack Architecture**: "Launch [format] at €[X] targeting [occasion/segment]"
3. **Promo Optimization**: "Shift promo from [deep TPR] to [event-led] in [category] to protect margin"
4. **Portfolio Rebalancing**: "Increase [segment] mix from [X]% to [Y]% to match category growth"
5. **Hero Renovation**: "Refresh [SKU] to recover [X]pp velocity vs 2 years ago"
6. **Tail Pruning**: "Delist bottom [N] SKUs (< €[X]K value) to fund [action]"
7. **Pricing**: "Adjust price index from [X] to [Y] to close gap with [competitor]"
8. **Channel Strategy**: "Develop [channel]-specific pack/price for Discount/Online"
