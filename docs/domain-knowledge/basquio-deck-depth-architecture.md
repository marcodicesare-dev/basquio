# Basquio Deck Depth Architecture

## Purpose
This knowledge pack defines what "deeper" means in long decks. More slides do not justify broader repetition. They justify a tighter MECE issue tree and a deeper drill-down cascade.

## Issue Tree Mandate
For decks with 20+ slides, plan the deck as an issue tree:

1. Root question: the brief's governing question.
2. Chapter branches: 3-6 mutually exclusive themes.
3. Leaf questions: one unique analytical question per slide.

Two slides cannot answer the same leaf question with different chart types. If a slide can be reconstructed from a prior slide, it is redundant.

## Drill-Down Dimension Catalog

| Dimension | Typical question answered | Suitable depth |
|---|---|---|
| Segment | Which segment is growing or shrinking? | 10+ slides |
| Channel | Where does the growth or loss happen? | 15+ slides |
| Format | Which pack or format drives the result? | 20+ slides |
| Brand x Segment | Which brands over- or under-index by segment? | 20+ slides |
| Brand x Channel | Where is the brand winning or losing? | 25+ slides |
| SKU Pareto | Which SKUs create most of the value or loss? | 30+ slides |
| Flavour / Variant | Which sub-range is driving the trend? | 30+ slides |
| Promo Intensity x Effectiveness | Is spend efficient or wasted? | 30+ slides |
| Price Ladder | Is the issue premiumization, parity, or price gap? | 30+ slides |
| Retailer-specific | Which retailer or banner is the real battleground? | 40+ slides |
| Occasion / Daypart | Which need-state is open or weak? | 50+ slides |
| Sensitivity / Scenario | What happens under prudent/base/ambitious assumptions? | 50+ slides |
| SKU Contribution Waterfall | Which SKUs explain the bridge? | 60+ slides |
| Shopper Cohort | Which households or cohorts are moving? | 70+ slides |

Coverage expectations:
- 20 slides: at least 4 dimensions
- 40 slides: at least 7 dimensions
- 60 slides: at least 10 dimensions
- 100 slides: at least 14 dimensions

## Progressive Disclosure Patterns
Long decks should deepen the question step by step.

Good pattern:
1. Category / segment: identify the problem pool.
2. Channel / format / brand: localize where it happens.
3. Driver: explain why it happens.
4. SKU / retailer / scenario: quantify what to do next.

Example:
- Slide 1: Share loss is concentrated in Reconstituted.
- Slide 2: Within Reconstituted, Super drives most of the decline.
- Slide 3: Within Super, three SKUs account for 70% of the drop.
- Slide 4: Those SKUs have a price-index issue, not a distribution issue.

## MECE Test For Slide Pairs
For any pair of analytical slides A and B:

1. Mutual exclusivity: A and B must not use the same data cut at the same decomposition level.
2. Collective contribution: removing either slide must lose information the reader cannot reconstruct from the other.

Quick question:
"If I only had slide A, would I already know slide B?"

If the answer is yes, collapse or deepen the pair.

## Banned Broadening Patterns
- Same finding shown as a bar chart, then a line chart, then a table.
- "Category overview", "market overview", and "category performance" as separate slides with the same point.
- Adjacent slides that both say the same segment is declining, without a deeper cut.
- Appendix slides that replay the body at a different chart type or aggregation.
- Layout variety used to mask analytical sameness.

## Recommendation Depth Contract
Recommendations are only credible after the deck reaches at least two evidence levels below the topline claim.

Example:
- L1: Segment underperforms.
- L2: Underperformance is concentrated in specific channels.
- L3: Within those channels, pricing or distribution explains the gap.

Do not jump from L1 directly to action.

## Authoring Reminder
Long decks earn their length by drilling deeper, not by saying the same thing more elegantly.
