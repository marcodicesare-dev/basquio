## 1. Kantar Worldpanel: Product Suite & Panel Infrastructure

### Panel Scale
- **130+** purchase and usage panels globally
- **4 million+** food and drinks occasions tracked
- **563 billion** brand choices tracked per year (also stated as "563m" on the Worldpanel by Numerator site — likely 563 million brand choices in a single market vs. 563 billion globally across all panels)
- **65 markets** covered (including partners)
- **15,000+** retailers tracked
- **50,000+** brands tracked
- **1,000** categories measured at "measurement grade"
- **6 billion** people represented worldwide (via panel projections)

### Six Solution Pillars
1. **Worldpanel Purchase** — continuous household purchase panels across all FMCG categories
2. **Worldpanel Usage** — occasion-based panels tracking every snack, beverage, and meal consumed (at home + on-the-go)
3. **Beauty & Fashion** — 360-degree individual behaviour across skin care, body care, cosmetics, hair, fragrance, male grooming, apparel, footwear
4. **Tech & Entertainment** — ComTech panels tracking smartphone, tablet, laptop, wearables, smart speakers, video/music streaming subscriptions
5. **PanelVoice Surveys** — targeted surveys of actual panellists (same people behind the purchase data), creating single-source attitudinal + behavioural data
6. **Behavioural Analytics & Simulators** — predictive analytics for assortment, pack size, NPD, marketing optimisation

### Unique Kantar Differentiator vs. NIQ
- **Single-source panel design:** The same panellists who generate purchase data can be surveyed (PanelVoice), creating a direct link between what consumers SAY and what they DO. NIQ's Homescan and survey products are typically separate samples.
- **Usage/occasion panels:** Kantar tracks actual consumption occasions (meals, snacks, beverages) not just purchase events. This enables demand-moment analysis that purchase-only panels cannot provide.
- **Demand Moments framework:** Kantar's proprietary concept of "micro-moments" in food and drink consumption — goes beyond purchase occasions to consumption contexts.

## 2. Core Metrics & KPIs (Kantar-Specific)

### Purchase Panel Metrics
| Metric | Definition | Analytical Use |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Penetration** | % of households/individuals who purchased a brand/category in a period | Size of buyer base; growth diagnostic |
| **Loyalty** | Share of category spend allocated to a brand by its buyers | Depth of relationship; retention health |
| **Repeat buyers** | Buyers who purchased 2+ times in a period | Retention signal; distinguishes trial from adoption |
| **New buyers** | First-time purchasers in a period | Trial/recruitment effectiveness |
| **Switchers** | Buyers who moved between brands across periods | Competitive vulnerability; opportunity sizing |
| **Loyalists** | Buyers who consistently purchase one brand | Core franchise health |
| **Brand Footprint** | Consumer Reach Points (CRP) = Penetration x Consumer Choice x Population | Kantar's proprietary ranking of most-chosen FMCG brands globally |

### Usage Panel Metrics
| Metric | Definition | Analytical Use |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Demand Moments** | Micro-occasions of food/drink consumption (time, location, need-state, social context) | Identifies white space for NPD and portfolio expansion |
| **Consumption occasions** | Individual eating/drinking events tracked by daypart, location, companions | Maps brand presence across the consumption landscape |
| **At-home vs. on-the-go** | Location dimension of consumption | Channel strategy; format/pack size decisions |
| **OOH (Out-of-Home) spend** | Expenditure on food/drink consumed outside the home (takeaways, cafes, food service) | Proven: OOH spend lifts across drinks and snacks do NOT cannibalise take-home sales |

### Retail & Channel Metrics
| Metric | Definition | Analytical Use |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| **Channel dynamics** | Shopper flows across store formats (hypermarket, supermarket, convenience, discounter, online) | Format strategy; distribution prioritisation |
| **Price sensitivity** | Responsiveness of purchase behaviour to price changes | Pricing strategy; promotion design |
| **Promotional effectiveness** | Incremental volume/value driven by promotional activity | Trade spend ROI |
| **Grocery Market Share** | Retailer share of total grocery spending by market | Competitive positioning for retailer negotiations |
| **Omnichannel shopping patterns** | Purchase behaviour tracked across online and offline channels | E-commerce strategy; click-and-collect sizing |

## 3. Taxonomies & Classification Systems

### Shopping Missions Taxonomy (Kantar Worldpanel)
Kantar classifies every shopping basket into mission types. This is a core differentiator — NIQ does not have an equivalent standardised mission classification at this granularity.

| Mission | Description | Price Premium vs. Main Shop | Typical Channel |
|---------|-------------|----------------------------|-----------------|
| **Main Shop / Trolley Shop** | Large stock-up trip; full weekly shop | Baseline (reference) | Hypermarket, Supermarket |
| **Top-Up** | Smaller replenishment trip between main shops | Moderate | Supermarket, Convenience |
| **For Tonight** | Same-day meal/occasion purchase | **+19%** per item vs. Main Shop | Convenience, Supermarket |
| **Specific Journey** | Purpose-driven trip (BBQ, Sunday dinner, celebration) | **+52%** per item vs. Main Shop | Varies by occasion |
| **Immediate Consumption** | Small baskets for instant use | High | Convenience, Food Service |

**Key trend (2023 data):** "Main Shop" share returned to 2013 levels after 7 years of gradual decline pre-lockdown. Fewer shoppers are visiting multiple stores. Implications: NPD focus shifting from specialisation to value proposition within single-store main shop.

### Category Roles (Industry Standard, used by Kantar in CatMan)
| Role | Definition | Strategic Implication |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Destination** | Category that drives store choice; shoppers specifically seek out | Invest heavily; competitive pricing; widest assortment |
| **Core / Routine** | Everyday categories that are expected to be available | Maintain strong availability; competitive pricing |
| **Convenience** | Categories that fill immediate needs; impulse-driven | Premium pricing acceptable; visibility critical |
| **Seasonal** | Categories with time-bound demand spikes | Promotional windowing; event-driven assortment |

### Occasion Types for Occasion-Based Marketing
| Occasion Type | Examples | Timing Pattern |
|--------------|----------|----------------|
| **Sporting events** | Olympics, Super Bowl | Food purchases 1 week in advance; event items 2+ weeks |
| **Cultural holidays** | Christmas, Easter, Thanksgiving | Long lead-time planning |
| **Gifting occasions** | Valentine's Day, Mother's/Father's Day | 2-3 weeks advance |
| **Seasonal events** | Back-to-School, Memorial Day, 4th of July | Varies by retailer (Walmart shoppers: 1 week; Target shoppers: 2 weeks) |
| **Entertainment** | Halloween, BBQ season | Category-specific timing |

**Key insight:** Food retailers win food-based events. Mass merchandisers win food + item events. Cross-category partnerships (e.g., food brand + supplies vendor) are effective for mixed-item occasions like Back-to-School.

## 4. Analytical Frameworks & Diagnostic Patterns

### 4.1 Kantar Brand Growth Diagnostic
Kantar's core growth framework decomposes brand performance into:
- **More buyers** (penetration growth) — primary growth lever
- **More occasions** (frequency/loyalty) — secondary lever
- **More spend per occasion** (premiumisation/upsizing) — tertiary lever

This maps to the Worldpanel data structure: Penetration x Frequency x Spend per Trip = Total Brand Revenue.

### 4.2 The 8-Step Category Management Process
Industry-standard framework heavily used in Kantar-supported retailer engagements:

1. **Define Categories** — Group products by consumer need/usage similarity
2. **Assess Roles** — Assign Destination / Core / Convenience / Seasonal role
3. **Assess Performance** — Periodic review; benchmark against retailer sales data and competitors
4. **Set Objectives & Targets** — Goals for margin, sales, volume; tracked via Category Scorecard
5. **Devise Strategies** — Per-category strategies:
   - Transaction Building Strategy (increase basket size)
   - Excitement Building Strategy (drive trial/engagement)
   - Image Enhancing Strategy (premiumisation/positioning)
6. **Set Tactics (4 P's):**
   - **Product (Assortment):** SKU count reflecting category role and retailer mission
   - **Price:** Price manipulation as strategy lever
   - **Placement (Shelf):** Planogram-driven; complementary adjacencies; size-based flow
   - **Promotions:** Promotional calendar aligned to natural purchase timelines
7. **Implement** — Execute via planograms; embody the category plan physically
8. **Review** — Continuous; reflect changing consumer environment

### 4.3 Category Scorecard
A tracking instrument for category objectives (Step 4 above). Monitors:
- Sales (value, volume, units)
- Margin
- Category growth vs. market
- Share of category by brand/supplier
- Promotional uplift
- Distribution metrics

### 4.5 Demand Moments Framework (Kantar Worldpanel Usage)
Kantar's proprietary approach to decomposing consumption:
- Every eating/drinking event is captured as a "moment" with attributes: time of day, location, social context, need-state, food/drink category, brand
- Enables identification of growing, declining, and emerging consumption moments
- Connects purchase data (what was bought) to usage data (when/how it was consumed)
- Closes the loop: purchase → consumption → motivations → brand choice

**Unique to Kantar:** NIQ does not have an equivalent continuous usage panel. NIQ's consumption data comes from periodic surveys or third-party partnerships, not longitudinal behavioural tracking.

### 4.6 Shopping Mission Analysis
Kantar's basket-level classification enables:
- **Mission mix by retailer:** Which retailer captures which missions? (e.g., discounter = main shop; convenience = top-up/for-tonight)
- **Price premium by mission:** Quantified premiums (19% for "For Tonight", 52% for "Specific Journey") justify premium NPD for specific missions
- **NPD strategy alignment:** Different missions demand different product propositions:
  - Main Shop: value-driven innovation (everyday solutions)
  - Top-Up: convenience formats, grab-and-go
  - For Tonight: premium, meal-solution, treat/reward
  - Specific Journey: occasion-specific bundles (BBQ packs, celebration ranges)

## 5. Unique Kantar Insights Not Available from NIQ

### 5.1 Purchase-to-Usage Loop
NIQ tracks what was bought. Kantar tracks what was bought AND how/when/where it was consumed. This is structurally different — it enables:
- **Waste analysis:** What is purchased but not consumed?
- **Substitution mapping:** What do consumers use instead of your product in specific occasions?
- **Portfolio white space:** Which consumption moments have no brand present?
- **Format optimisation:** Which pack sizes match which usage occasions?

### 5.2 OOH (Out-of-Home) vs. Take-Home Dynamics
Kantar tracks both in-store purchases and out-of-home consumption (cafes, restaurants, takeaways, food service). Proven insight: OOH spend growth in drinks and snacks does NOT cannibalise take-home sales — they are additive. NIQ primarily covers retail sell-through; OOH is a separate data universe.

### 5.3 Single-Source Attitudinal + Behavioural
PanelVoice surveys the exact same people whose purchases are tracked. This eliminates the sample-matching problem that plagues separate survey + panel approaches. You can directly correlate "consumers who say X" with "consumers who buy Y."

### 5.4 Beauty & Personal Care Usage Panels
Kantar tracks personal care routines (not just purchases): what products are used, in what order, how frequently, by whom. This is uniquely valuable for BPC categories where purchase frequency and usage frequency diverge significantly.

### 5.5 Brand Footprint Ranking
Kantar's proprietary global ranking using Consumer Reach Points (CRP = Penetration x Consumer Choice x Population). Published annually. The only standardised cross-market measure of how often consumers around the world choose specific FMCG brands.

### 5.6 Grocery Market Share Visualisation
Kantar publishes near-real-time grocery market share data by retailer in multiple markets. This is the reference dataset for grocery retailer competitive tracking in the UK and several European/LatAm markets (where NIQ Scantrack is less dominant).

## 8. Data Quality & Methodology Notes

- **Panel-based vs. POS-based:** Kantar's core FMCG data is panel-based (representative household samples recording all purchases). NIQ's Scantrack/RMS is POS-based (retailer barcode scans). Panel data captures WHO bought and cross-category baskets; POS data captures exact transaction volumes at store level.
- **Measurement grade:** Kantar claims "measurement grade data in 1,000 categories" — this implies sufficient sample sizes for robust category-level reads, not just top-level aggregates.
- **Worldpanel by Numerator:** As of 2026, Kantar's Worldpanel division operates as "Worldpanel by Numerator" following the Numerator acquisition/partnership. The London HQ (Vivo Building, 30 Stamford St, London SE1 9LQ) is the global office.

## 9. Actionable Patterns for FMCG Analysis

### When analysing Kantar data, prioritise:
1. **Penetration as the primary growth lever** — Kantar's research consistently shows penetration gain is the #1 driver of brand growth (aligns with Ehrenberg-Bass Institute findings)
2. **Mission-level basket analysis** — decompose performance by shopping mission, not just channel
3. **Usage occasions as white space** — look for consumption moments where no brand is present
4. **Price premium by mission** — use the 19%/52% benchmarks to validate premium NPD positioning
5. **Cross-category basket composition** — what else is in the basket when your brand is purchased?
6. **Loyalty decomposition** — separate repeat buyers, loyalists, and switchers; each requires different strategy
7. **OOH as additive** — do not treat out-of-home consumption as cannibalising retail sales

### Red flags in Kantar data interpretation:
- Penetration growth without loyalty growth = trial without adoption (leaky bucket)
- High loyalty without penetration growth = shrinking franchise (niche trap)
- Mission shift from Main Shop to Top-Up = potential value erosion for the retailer
- Declining consumption occasions with stable purchases = potential waste/stockpiling
