| Path to $10M ARR | Need 10,000–30,000 users (very hard) | Need 100–400 accounts (achievable) |
| Your unfair advantage | Limited — individual analysts find tools themselves | **MASSIVE** — your team IS the buyer and the user, with warm intros to hundreds of accounts |

| Margin | High but low absolute $ | High and high absolute $ |

> **THE VERDICT:** B2B, targeting teams of 5–20 analysts/category managers at CPG brands and market research firms. **BUT** with a self-serve PLG entry point (Pro tier at $29/month) so individual analysts can discover Basquio, fall in love with it, and pull it into their team. ### 2.2 Why Vertical-First (CPG Market Research) Beats Horizontal

The temptation is to go broad: "intelligence-first presentations for everyone." Resist it.

1. **You OWN the domain.** Your team knows CPG market research data formats (NielsenIQ, Circana, Kantar), workflows, and deliverable expectations. Going vertical lets you build features that are 10x better for this specific use case than any horizontal tool.

2. **Vertical = faster sales.** When Alessandro demos Basquio to a NielsenIQ colleague, the colleague instantly understands the value because they live the same pain. No education needed. No "what is this tool?" — just "how do I get access?"

3. **Vertical = higher prices.** Generic AI presentation tools charge $8–30/month. A tool purpose-built for CPG syndicated data → insight decks can charge $5,000–8,000/analyst/year ($400–670/month) because the ROI is measured against $78K+/year in analyst time savings.

4. **Vertical = defensible moat.** Gamma and Beautiful.ai will never build NielsenIQ data parsing, category management templates, or syndicated data intelligence features. They're horizontal. You're the only one building this.

5. **Vertical = referral engine.** CPG market research is a tight community. NielsenIQ analysts talk to each other. Brand-side research managers attend the same conferences (IRI/Circana Summit, CAGNY, Expo West). One happy customer = 5 warm referrals.

You can go horizontal LATER (Year 2+). But the fastest path to revenue is owning the CPG market research niche first. This is the Veeva playbook (CRM for pharma → $40B company) and the Procore playbook (project management for construction → $12B company). Vertical specificity wins.

## 8. Why Not B2C (And When to Add It)

### The B2C Case

- Individual market research analysts, freelance consultants, and independent strategy advisors would pay $29–99/month for a tool that automates report creation.
- PLG distribution: analyst discovers Basquio, signs up, generates a deck, shares it with their team/client, team adopts.
- Lower friction: no procurement, no security review, no committee decision.
- Content marketing + Product Hunt + LinkedIn can drive thousands of signups.

### Why B2B First

- **Your team's network is B2B.** You know team leads, directors, and VPs at NielsenIQ, Mondelez, and Victorinox. You don't know 10,000 individual analysts.
- **Revenue math:** 1 team deal ($25–50K ACV) = 70–170 individual Pro subscriptions ($290/year). You close deals faster with your network.
- **Product feedback is better from teams.** Individual users give you feature requests. Teams give you workflow requirements — which is what builds defensible products.
- **Enterprise logos unlock fundraising.** "Mondelez uses Basquio" is worth more to investors than "500 individual analysts use Basquio."

### When to Layer in B2C

Month 3–4, after you've proven the product with team accounts:

- Free tier (3 reports/month) drives top-of-funnel
- Pro tier ($29/month) captures individual analysts
- Analysts who love it pull Basquio into their teams (bottom-up adoption)
- This becomes your inbound pipeline for team and enterprise deals

The correct model is: **B2B-first (months 1–3) → Add PLG/B2C layer (months 3–6) → Both flywheels spinning (months 6+).** This is how Figma, Notion, and Slack all scaled: enterprise deals for revenue, PLG for distribution.
